## Document Boundary

This document maps how the 30 candidate capability themes approved for exploration in Global
Platform Discovery Baseline v0.1.1 may logically collaborate. It does not approve architecture,
Components, Domains, bounded contexts, services, ownership, canonical facts, write models,
Contracts, Events, persistence, infrastructure, runtime, deployment, frameworks, technology, or
implementation.

The controlling Discovery baseline is:

```text
00-GLOBAL-PLATFORM-DISCOVERY.md
  +
00A-GLOBAL-PLATFORM-DISCOVERY-PATCH-v0.1.1.md
  =
Global Platform Discovery Baseline v0.1.1
```

The Patch controls repository authority and readiness: Genesis
`20-PLATFORM-ECOSYSTEM.md` is the complete ecosystem authority, no Genesis 21 artifact is
required, and the Discovery is ready for this mapping phase. All other Discovery content remains
unchanged.

In this map:

- a **candidate Capability** is one existing `GPCT` Discovery theme, not an approved platform
  Capability;
- a **candidate Domain relationship area** is an analytical grouping, not a Domain, bounded
  context, owner, service, or deployment unit;
- a **flow** is logical ordering for understanding only, not a runtime sequence;
- an **input** is an owner-controlled fact or context reference, not transferred data ownership;
- an **output** is a possible logical result to be evaluated by Proposal, not a canonical artifact;
- a **responsibility** is a question about work, not an ownership assignment; and
- all existing owners and frozen boundaries remain external authorities.

## 1. Mission Flow

### 1.1 Logical Flow 1 — Mission Flow

```text
Authorized Workspace and Business need
  ↓
Identify current tenant, organization, resource, and purpose context
  ↓
Identify applicable Region, Country, language, timezone, currency, address,
jurisdiction, legal, compliance, privacy, and residency questions
  ↓
Preserve canonical source owners and exact applicable references
  ↓
Relate candidate global concerns to Marketplace, intelligence, AI,
Commerce OS, and other independent Operating Systems
  ↓
Identify presentation, applicability, collaboration, projection,
administration, operational, migration, and scale implications
  ↓
Return each consequential interpretation or action to its frozen owner
  ↓
Preserve uncertainty, conflict, provenance, authorization, and Audit participation
```

The Mission Flow never implies a Global Platform write model, a tenant above Workspace, an
approved Region or Country model, a new organization level, a shared cross-OS writer, automatic
target configuration, a duplicate frozen owner, a global transaction, global ordering, or
implementation order.

## 5. Decision Flow

### 5.1 Logical Flow 5 — Decision Flow

“Decision” here means a logical progression for determining what must be considered. It does not
redefine the canonical Business Brain Decision.

```text
Identify the business or user need
  ↓
Resolve current Workspace, Business, organization, actor, resource, and purpose scope
  ↓
Identify the canonical owner of every required fact
  ↓
Identify potentially applicable Region, Country, locale, language, timezone,
currency, address, legal, jurisdiction, compliance, privacy, and residency context
  ↓
Identify conflicts, ambiguity, missing context, stale context, and inherited deferrals
  ↓
Identify which frozen owner must validate, decide, recommend, configure, or act
  ↓
Preserve human approval and owner-domain authorization where consequential
  ↓
Produce only an authorized owner result or non-canonical projection
  ↓
Preserve evidence, explanation, and Audit participation
```

The Proposal must decide whether any reusable global decision concept is required. This map does
not approve one.

| unresolved issue | missing context, ambiguity, conflict, source gap, or deferred choice | Proposal, ADR, or later approved milestone lifecycle |

These outputs do not establish a Global Platform canonical write responsibility.

## 15. Global Responsibilities

At Capability Map stage, “Global Responsibilities” means candidate logical work to be evaluated,
not approved ownership. Candidate work includes:

- interpreting which explicit source-owned contexts may matter to a global interaction;
- preserving the Workspace tenant boundary and any authorized narrower scope;
- relating localization and internationalization needs to canonical owner values;
- exposing Country, Region, legal, tax-jurisdiction, compliance, privacy, and residency questions;
- coordinating questions across frozen owners without transferring their authority;
- preserving independent Operating System usability and optional cross-OS integration;
- carrying relevant context to Marketplace, Business Brain, Recommendation, Configuration,
  Knowledge, AI Coordinator, Commerce OS, and future OS owners;
- supporting visible Navigation context and governed administration questions;
- identifying authorized Search, Reporting, Analytics, and operational-observation needs; and
- preserving provenance, uncertainty, version, compatibility, Audit participation, migration, and
  change-control questions.

Whether Global Platform owns any of this work is explicitly deferred to Proposal.

## 16. Global Non-Responsibilities

Global Platform candidate capabilities never imply responsibility to:

- replace Governance, Genesis, Accepted ADRs, or any Freeze;
- create a tenant above Workspace or typed Workspaces;
- redefine Workspace, Business, Business Unit, Department, Branch, identity, Membership,
  Permission, Delegation, Settings, or organization hierarchy;
- own Business DNA, Knowledge, Rules, Decisions, Recommendations, Configuration Proposals,
  Marketplace Assets, AI Expert Definitions, or Operating System facts;
- contribute AI output to canonical Decision formation;
- create parallel Marketplace, Commerce, AI Coordinator, Business Brain, Recommendation,
  Configuration, Knowledge, Search, Reporting, Analytics, Audit, or Observability truth;
- make one Operating System depend on another for its core workflow;
- infer authorization, hide current context, or bypass human approval for consequential action;
- treat localization, aggregation, Reporting, Search, Analytics, or operational evidence as
  canonical ownership;
- select external authorities, technologies, providers, runtime mechanisms, persistence,
  deployment, or implementation; or
- resolve any Discovery open question.

## 17. Collaboration with Frozen Authorities and Milestones

All collaboration described here is logical and candidate-only. It introduces no interface,
mechanism, owner, or approved dependency.

### 17.1 Governance

Governance supplies authority precedence, canonical terminology, ADR discipline, milestone
lifecycle, versioning, review, freeze, and change control. Candidate global concerns must be
classified through those processes; this map cannot approve them.

### 17.2 Genesis

Genesis v1.1 supplies platform intent and ecosystem foundations. Genesis
`20-PLATFORM-ECOSYSTEM.md` is the complete approved ecosystem authority for this Discovery
baseline. The map neither requires nor infers a Genesis 21 document.

### 17.3 Core Platform

Candidate global concerns consume Core-owned identity, Workspace, organization, Membership,
Permission, Settings, Localization, Navigation, Product Hub, AI Coordinator, Audit, Search,
Observability, and shared platform context. Core remains the sole authority for those concepts;
this map only identifies where global applicability may need to be visible.

### 17.4 Business Brain

Candidate global context may become source-attributed input to Business Brain. Business Brain
alone completes its canonical deterministic Decision; AI remains downstream; and Business DNA,
Knowledge, Rules, Recommendation, and Configuration ownership remain separate.

### 17.5 Commerce OS

Candidate global context may inform Commerce-owned setup, tax, documents, currency, addresses,
payments, privacy, reports, and operations. Commerce OS remains independently usable and retains
all canonical Commerce facts, writes, aggregates, and lifecycles.

### 17.6 Marketplace

Candidate global context may affect discovery, availability, compatibility, licensing,
distribution, entitlement, installation, activation, applicability, support, and presentation.
Marketplace retains its shared Asset model, Workspace-scoped state, publication-path ownership,
commercial facts, and every Marketplace lifecycle.

### 17.7 AI Expert Network

Candidate global context may affect AI Expert eligibility, language, country, provider, privacy,
residency, evidence, and safety. The Core AI Coordinator remains the single interaction
coordinator, and frozen publication-path Definition ownership remains unchanged.

### 17.8 Future Operating Systems

Future Operating Systems may consume authorized global context while keeping their own
subscriptions, navigation, permissions, workflows, facts, and lifecycles. No candidate global
capability may become a prerequisite for an OS's standalone core workflow unless a future
approved architecture explicitly changes that guarantee.

## 20. Logical Decision Lifecycle

```text

  ↓

  ↓

  ↓
```

## 21. Dependency Lifecycle

### 21.1 Logical Flow 11 — Dependency Lifecycle

```text
Candidate dependency need identified
  ↓

  ↓

  ↓

  ↓

  ↓
```

Dependency loss must not silently transfer ownership, merge tenants, permit stale authority, or
