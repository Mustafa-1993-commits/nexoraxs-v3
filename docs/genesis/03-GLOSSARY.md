# Nexoraxs Glossary

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Foundation

---

## Business

A real-world organization, activity, or operation that delivers value to customers and needs systems to manage people, processes, products, services, money, and decisions.

## Workspace

The top-level Nexoraxs account that owns users, subscriptions, businesses, permissions, settings, and enabled operating systems.

## Business Unit

A specific business identity inside a workspace. A workspace may contain one or many business units.

## Branch

A physical or operational location that belongs to a business unit.

## Operating System / OS

A product area that manages a major business function, such as Commerce OS, HR OS, CRM OS, or Healthcare OS.

## Capability

A reusable business ability such as inventory, barcode, expiry tracking, delivery, payroll, CRM, appointments, fleet, or manufacturing.

## Module

A product implementation of one or more capabilities inside an OS.

## Industry

A business category such as pharmacy, restaurant, clinic, factory, or construction. In Nexoraxs, an industry is not a hard-coded product. It is a knowledge profile composed from capabilities, workflows, rules, terminology, and recommendations.

## Business DNA

The structured profile that describes how a business works. It includes identity, industry, size, stage, capabilities, branches, employees, sales channels, compliance, goals, risks, and growth plan.

## Knowledge Engine

The structured knowledge layer that stores industries, capabilities, rules, questions, defaults, workflows, recommendations, KPIs, reports, automations, terminology, country rules, and compliance profiles.

## Business Brain

The intelligence layer that uses Business DNA and the Knowledge Engine to recommend products, capabilities, plans, workflows, dashboards, and next actions.

## Recommendation

An explainable suggestion made by Nexoraxs to help a business operate better.

## Integration

A connection between two OS products for the same workspace. Integrations are optional and must not make one OS dependent on another.
