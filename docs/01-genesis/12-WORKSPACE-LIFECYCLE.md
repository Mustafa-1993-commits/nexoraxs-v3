# Workspace Lifecycle

Version: 1.0

Status: Foundation

Owner: Nexoraxs

---

# Purpose

This document defines the complete lifecycle of a Workspace inside Nexoraxs.

The Workspace is the customer's home.

Everything begins here.

Businesses.

Operating Systems.

Users.

Permissions.

Subscriptions.

Knowledge Context.

AI Context.

Everything belongs to the Workspace.

---

# Definition

A Workspace represents one customer account.

It is the highest organizational boundary inside Nexoraxs.

A Workspace is not a company.

A Workspace can manage one or many businesses.

---

# Core Principles

A Workspace owns Businesses.

Businesses own Business Units.

Business Units own Branches.

Business Units organize Departments as internal organizational subdivisions.

Every Business owns separate Business DNA.

Operating Systems operate on Business Units.

Knowledge belongs to the Platform.

# Stage 1
## Workspace Created

Minimal information only.

Examples

Workspace Name

Owner

Country

Language

Timezone

No complex configuration.

The mandatory Workspace entitlement is created.

It is not independently billed during MVP.

---

# Stage 2
## Business Identity and Business Architect

The Workspace has no software yet.

The customer creates a Business inside the Workspace.

The Business Architect interviews the customer about that Business.

Core Business DNA is created for that Business only.

Each additional Business owns separate Business DNA.

---

# Stage 3
## Core Workspace Ready

Business Brain prepares:

Capability Recommendations

Permission Recommendations

Template Recommendations

Dashboard Recommendations

Report Recommendations

Automation Recommendations

Mapped Operating System Implementation Options

The Workspace is now Core Workspace Ready and may enter Product Hub.

No Operating System is implied to be ready.

---

# Stage 4
## Select and Prepare an Operating System

In Product Hub, the customer selects an Operating System and a plan.

The selection creates the Operating System subscription and begins installation.

The selected Operating System owns its setup experience.

Setup selects or creates the operational Business Unit inside the Business.

After setup, configuration, and activation, the Operating System becomes Operating System Ready.

Operating Systems may include:

Commerce OS

HR OS

CRM OS

Projects OS

Healthcare OS

Fleet OS

Accounting OS

Installation should take seconds.

---

# Stage 5
## Daily Operations

Workspace becomes operational.

The customer enters the Operational Dashboard of an Operating System that is Operating System Ready.

Users collaborate.

Business Brain starts learning.

---
