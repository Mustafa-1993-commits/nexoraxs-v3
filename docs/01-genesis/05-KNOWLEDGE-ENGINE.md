# Knowledge Engine

Version: 1.0

Status: Foundation

Owner: Nexoraxs

---

# Purpose

The Knowledge Engine is the central source of business knowledge inside Nexoraxs.

It stores business expertise, best practices, business rules, terminology, workflows, KPIs, recommendations, and configuration data.

Every Operating System, AI service, Recommendation Engine, and Business Brain consumes knowledge from this engine.

Knowledge is the foundation.

Everything else is built on top of it.

---

# Vision

Business knowledge should exist independently from software.

Software changes.

Knowledge grows.

Knowledge must survive software generations.

---

# Core Principles

## Knowledge First

Knowledge always exists before implementation.

Software is generated from knowledge.

Never the opposite.

---

## Platform Wide

Knowledge belongs to Nexoraxs Platform.

No Operating System owns business knowledge.

Commerce OS, HR OS, CRM OS and every future OS consume the same knowledge source.

Knowledge content remains shared across the platform.

Workspace or Business applicability never creates a duplicate copy of that content.

---

## Structured

Knowledge is structured.

It is not random documentation.

Every knowledge object follows a standard model.

---

## Explainable

Every recommendation must point back to the knowledge that generated it.

Knowledge must always be traceable.

# Knowledge Object

Every knowledge object contains:

- ID
- Name
- Category
- Description
- Tags
- Country Scope
- Industry Scope
- Capability Scope
- Related Objects
- Source
- Version
- Status
- Owner
- Last Updated

Every published version is immutable.

A change creates a new version instead of modifying the published version.

# Knowledge Lifecycle

Draft

↓

Review

↓

Approved

↓

Published

↓

Deprecated

↓

Archived

Knowledge is versioned.

Knowledge is never deleted.

Published versions are immutable.

Deprecation and archival preserve every earlier version.

---

# Ownership

Knowledge belongs to the platform.

Operating Systems cannot duplicate knowledge.

They reference it.

Workspace and Business contexts may activate and apply shared knowledge.

Activation and applicability never transfer ownership or duplicate the knowledge asset.

# Golden Rule

If the same business knowledge appears in more than one place,

the architecture is wrong.

Knowledge must exist once.

Everyone else consumes it.
