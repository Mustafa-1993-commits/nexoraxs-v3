# Customer Journey

Version: 1.0

Status: Foundation

Owner: Nexoraxs

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official customer journey inside Nexoraxs.

The Customer Journey explains how a customer moves from first contact to an intelligent, operating workspace that grows with their business over time.

This journey must guide product decisions, UX decisions, onboarding flows, AI behavior, recommendations, and Operating System activation.

---

# Core Principle

The customer should never feel they are configuring software.

The customer should feel Nexoraxs is understanding their business and preparing the right operating system for them.

---

# Journey Philosophy

Nexoraxs is not a traditional onboarding wizard.

Nexoraxs is a business conversation.

Every step should either:

- Understand the business
- Reduce customer effort
- Recommend the next best action
- Configure the platform
- Explain why a recommendation matters

If a step does not do one of these things, it should not exist.

---

# Journey Stages

The customer journey is divided into stages.

---

## Stage 1
### Discovery

The customer discovers Nexoraxs through:

- Website
- Referral
- Demo
- Sales conversation
- Marketplace
- Social media
- Partner

The goal of this stage is to communicate one idea:

Describe your business.

We'll build its operating system.

---

## Stage 2
### Account Creation

The customer creates an account.

Required information should be minimal.

Examples:

- Name
- Email
- Password
- Preferred language

Nexoraxs must not ask business configuration questions before trust is established.

---

## Stage 3
### Workspace Creation

The customer creates a Workspace.

A Workspace represents the customer account boundary.

Examples:

- Mustafa Group
- ABC Retail
- Delta Medical

The Workspace owns:

- Users
- Businesses
- Subscriptions
- Settings
- AI Context
- Knowledge Context

---

## Stage 4
### Business Understanding

Nexoraxs begins understanding the customer's business.

This is where Business DNA starts.

The platform asks simple, business-focused questions.

Examples:

- What type of business do you run?
- How many branches do you have?
- Do you sell products, services, or both?
- Do you sell in-store, online, or both?
- What is your main goal right now?

Questions must feel conversational.

Not technical.

---

## Stage 5
### Business DNA Generation

Nexoraxs transforms customer answers into Business DNA.

Business DNA describes how the business operates.

It does not describe which software the customer selected.

Business DNA becomes the foundation for recommendations.

---

## Stage 6
### Capability Mapping

Business Brain maps Business DNA to required capabilities.

Examples:

A pharmacy may require:

- Inventory
- Barcode
- Expiry Tracking
- Batch Tracking
- POS
- Suppliers

A restaurant may require:

- POS
- Kitchen
- Tables
- Delivery
- Inventory
- Reservations

A mobile store may require:

- POS
- Inventory
- IMEI Tracking
- Warranty
- Repairs
- Installments

---

## Stage 7
### Recommendations

The Recommendation Engine recommends:

- Capabilities
- Operating Systems
- Plans
- Workflows
- Dashboards
- Reports
- Automations
- AI Experts

Every recommendation must explain:

- Why it is recommended
- What problem it solves
- What happens if ignored
- What benefit the customer can expect

---

## Stage 8
### Operating System Selection

The customer sees recommended Operating Systems.

Examples:

- Commerce OS
- CRM OS
- HR OS
- Healthcare OS
- Manufacturing OS

The customer may accept, reject, or postpone any recommendation.

Operating Systems are never forced.

---

## Stage 9
### Plan Selection

The customer selects a plan.

Plan recommendations should be based on:

- Business size
- Number of branches
- Number of users
- Required capabilities
- Growth stage
- Usage expectations

The platform should explain why a plan is recommended.

---

## Stage 10
### Auto Configuration

After selection, Nexoraxs configures the workspace.

Configuration may include:

- Default dashboards
- Default reports
- Permissions
- Workflows
- Templates
- Capability settings
- Branch structure
- AI context
- Notifications

The customer should feel the workspace is ready.

Not empty.

---

## Stage 11
### First Value Moment

The customer must reach value quickly.

Examples:

- First product added
- First sale created
- First customer added
- First dashboard viewed
- First recommendation accepted

The platform must guide the customer toward this moment.

---

## Stage 12
### Guided Activation

Nexoraxs guides the customer through activation.

Examples:

- Complete profile
- Add first branch
- Add first user
- Import products
- Configure taxes
- Invite team
- Activate POS

Activation should be adaptive.

Different businesses require different activation paths.

---

## Stage 13
### Continuous Guidance

After setup, Nexoraxs continues guiding the customer.

Examples:

- You have not enabled inventory alerts.
- Your business may benefit from CRM.
- Your sales increased; consider analytics.
- Your team is growing; consider HR.

Guidance should be helpful.

Not annoying.

---

## Stage 14
### Growth Journey

As the business grows, Nexoraxs recommends new capabilities.

Examples:

Startup:

- Basic POS
- Basic Inventory

Growing:

- CRM
- Analytics
- Multi-branch

Scaling:

- HR
- Automation
- Advanced permissions

Enterprise:

- Advanced reporting
- Approvals
- Marketplace packs
- AI Experts

---

## Stage 15
### Multi-OS Expansion

A customer may start with one Operating System.

Later, they may add another.

Example:

Commerce OS

↓

CRM OS

↓

HR OS

Each Operating System must work independently.

Integrations are optional.

---

## Stage 16
### Long-Term Intelligence

Over time, Nexoraxs becomes more useful.

It learns from:

- Usage patterns
- Accepted recommendations
- Rejected recommendations
- Business outcomes
- Anonymous platform data
- Knowledge updates

Learning improves future recommendations.

Learning never modifies official knowledge without review.

---

# Journey Success Criteria

The journey succeeds when the customer says:

Nexoraxs understood my business.

The workspace is ready.

I know what to do next.

The system feels built for me.

---

# Journey Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- Long technical forms
- Empty dashboards
- Generic onboarding
- Unexplained recommendations
- Forced Operating Systems
- Too many decisions at once
- Asking what can be inferred
- Showing advanced settings too early

---

# Golden Rule

The customer journey must reduce uncertainty.

Every step should make the customer more confident that Nexoraxs understands their business.
