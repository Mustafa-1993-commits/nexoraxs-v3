# ADR-007: Capabilities Precede Industries and Software

## Status

Accepted

## Context

Industry-specific applications create duplication and cannot represent Businesses that combine models, channels, and operational needs in different ways.

## Decision

Capabilities are canonical, reusable, platform-wide descriptions of what a Business needs. Industries are combinations of Capabilities and never define software boundaries. The Business Brain recommends business improvements and Capabilities before mapping Operating Systems, plans, or Marketplace assets.

## Consequences

- Capability definitions remain independent, composable, and reusable.
- Industry and Sub Industry describe Business DNA and Knowledge applicability.
- No separate architecture is created for each industry.

## Alternatives Considered

- Hardcode industries as applications or Workspace types.
- Make an Operating System or industry own Capability definitions.
- Recommend industries instead of Capabilities.
